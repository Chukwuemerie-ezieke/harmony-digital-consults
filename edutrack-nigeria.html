EduTrack Nigeria

Real-time school operational excellence dashboard — monitor education metrics, track institutional performance, and make data-driven decisions.

What It Does

EduTrack Nigeria is a school management dashboard that brings operational intelligence to Nigerian educational institutions. It aggregates data from trusted sources including the World Bank Education API, UBEC statistics, and state-level education programmes to provide administrators with real-time visibility into school performance. Track attendance trends, enrollment patterns, infrastructure readiness, and academic outcomes — all from a single, intuitive dashboard.

For School Administrators

Monitor daily operations, attendance, enrollment, and compliance metrics. Get alerts on issues that need attention and track improvement over time.

For Education Planners

Access aggregated education data for planning, resource allocation, and policy decisions. Compare performance across schools, terms, and regions.

For State/District Officials

Bird's-eye view of multiple schools with comparative analytics, trend analysis, and performance benchmarks aligned to national education goals.

Features

Key Features

Built for the realities of Nigerian education — real data, real schools, real results.

Real-Time Dashboard

Live metrics on attendance, enrollment, academic performance, and infrastructure status with visual trend indicators.

Multi-Tenant Architecture

Supports multiple schools under one platform, each with isolated data and role-based access control.

Nigerian Curriculum Aligned

Built around the Nigerian education system — WAEC, NECO, JAMB frameworks, 6-3-3-4 structure, and state-specific requirements.

World Bank Data Integration

Pulls real education data from World Bank Education API, UBEC, EdoBEST, and PLANE programme datasets.

Attendance Tracking

Daily, weekly, and termly attendance monitoring with automated alerts for absenteeism patterns and compliance reporting.

Reports & Analytics

Generate institutional reports for UBEC submissions, board meetings, parent briefings, and regulatory compliance.
