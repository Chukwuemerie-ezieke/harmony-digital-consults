Visual Content — Slides, Images, Posters & Infographics

Turn a lesson plan into a 10-slide deck in 60 seconds. Generate a classroom poster in 90. Create an infographic without ever opening Photoshop. This unit is where AI starts to feel like magic.

⏱ 50 minutes 📊 Intermediate 🎯 4 learning objectives

Learning objectives

  1. Generate classroom visuals using Canva AI, Gamma, and image generators.
  2. Create lesson slides from a lesson plan in under 5 minutes.
  3. Design posters and infographics for classroom walls.
  4. Understand image rights, watermarks, and AI-disclosure responsibilities.

Why visuals matter

Retention multiplies when learners see + hear + do. A lesson with a single relevant image is recalled twice as well as one delivered by voice alone. AI now removes the design bottleneck.

Gamma — lesson plan → slides in 60 seconds

  1. Open gamma.app (free tier exists).
  2. Paste your lesson plan (the one you generated in Unit 3).
  3. Click Generate. Choose a theme.
  4. 60 seconds later, you have an 8–12 slide deck with titles, bullets, and matching imagery.
  5. Edit any slide inline — Gamma's editor is as easy as Google Slides.
  6. Export as PPTX, PDF, or present directly from the browser.

💡 Pro tip

Before pasting, add this line at the top of your text: "Create slides for a JSS1 classroom in Nigeria. Use simple English, large bullet points, and African imagery where possible."

Canva Magic Design — posters and worksheets

Canva's AI features (free with a Canva account) cover most teacher visuals:

  • Magic Design: type a description, get 4 design options. e.g. "Classroom poster about water cycle for primary school, colourful, African children, simple English."
  • Magic Write: AI writing inside any text box — useful for shortening or rewriting captions.
  • Magic Switch: turn a poster into a worksheet, or a worksheet into a social post.

You get a printable PDF or PNG. No Photoshop required.

Image generation — DALL·E, Gemini, Designer

For custom images, try:

  • ChatGPT (DALL·E built in for Plus users)
  • Gemini (free image generation)
  • Microsoft Designer / Copilot (free with a Microsoft account)

Good for: simple diagrams, scenes, characters, story illustrations, posters, decorations.

Weak at: scientifically accurate diagrams (heart, atom, plant cell), accurate maps, and any image with lots of text inside it. Always verify.

Image ethics for classrooms

  • Never generate images of real students.
  • Disclose to students when an image is AI-generated, especially in History or Science.
  • Don't generate images of real, identifiable people without permission.
  • Verify scientific and historical accuracy before using a generated image as fact.
  • Where possible, prefer images that reflect your learners' world — African children, local landscapes, familiar objects.
