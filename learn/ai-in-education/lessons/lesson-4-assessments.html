Worksheets, Quizzes & Assessments

Build three quizzes — foundation, standard, advanced — with answer keys, mark schemes, and Bloom's tagging, all in the time it takes to brew tea.

⏱ 55 minutes 📊 Intermediate 🎯 4 learning objectives

Learning objectives

  1. Generate differentiated worksheets in seconds.
  2. Build quizzes with answer keys and marking schemes.
  3. Create rubrics for projects and essays.
  4. Align questions to Bloom's Taxonomy levels.

Question types AI handles well

  • Fill-in-the-blank
  • Matching (column A ↔ column B)
  • True / False
  • Multiple choice (3, 4, or 5 options)
  • Short answer
  • Essay / extended response
  • Problem sets (especially Maths and Science)
  • Comprehension passages with questions

Just name the mix you want — for example, "5 MCQ + 3 short answer + 2 essay."

Bloom's Taxonomy — let AI do the tagging

Bloom's six levels move from simple recall to deep thinking:

LevelVerb examples
Rememberlist, name, recall, define
Understandexplain, summarise, describe
Applysolve, demonstrate, use
Analysecompare, contrast, classify
Evaluatejudge, justify, defend
Createdesign, propose, compose

Add this to your prompt: "Tag each question with its Bloom's Taxonomy level." AI will label every single one.

Always request the answer key

The single biggest time waster is generating a quiz, then sitting down to mark it yourself. Just say:

"Provide a complete answer key. For essay questions, give a model answer and a 4-point marking rubric. Total the marks at the end."

One prompt — quiz + answers + marks — done.

Rubrics in 30 seconds

Marking projects fairly is hard. Rubrics make it consistent. Prompt:

"Create a 4-point rubric for a JSS3 Social Studies project on community leadership. Criteria: content accuracy, organisation, examples used, presentation. Levels: Beginning (1), Developing (2), Proficient (3), Distinguished (4). Use simple descriptors."

You get a printable marking grid that you can show students before they start — so they know exactly what success looks like.

Tools beyond ChatGPT

  • Diffit — reading passages adapted to multiple grade levels.
  • MagicSchool AI — pre-built templates (quiz, IEP, parent letter, rubric).
  • Quizizz AI — turns text into an interactive quiz game.
  • Canva Magic Write — designs a printable worksheet straight onto an A4 layout.

Don't subscribe to all of them. Pick one general (ChatGPT or Gemini) and one teacher-specific (MagicSchool or Diffit). That's enough.

🎬 Demo — Quiz, answers, marks in one go

Prompt: "Create a 10-question quiz for SS2 Government on the topic Federalism. Mix 5 multiple choice, 3 short answer, and 2 essay questions. Tag each question with its Bloom's Taxonomy level. Provide an answer key and a marking scheme totalling 30 marks."

Result: complete quiz, every question tagged, answer key beneath, total marks at the bottom — all in 40 seconds.

Then say: "Now make an easier version for slower learners and a harder version for the top stream." Two more quizzes in another 40 seconds.
