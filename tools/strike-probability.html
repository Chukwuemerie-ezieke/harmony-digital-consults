Methodology \u2014 how the number is built

  1. Months since last salary review (weight 20%) \u2014 strike risk rises sharply past month 12 without an upward salary adjustment.
  2. FAAC / federal education allocation trend (weight 15%) \u2014 a falling % share signals budget tension; underfunding precedes most strikes.
  3. FG\u2013ASUU dialogue temperature (weight 20%) \u2014 communiqu\u00e9 tone since the last NEC meeting: 0 = active talks, 100 = breakdown.
  4. NLC / SSANU / NASU alignment (weight 15%) \u2014 strikes lengthen and are more likely to be called when allied unions echo the demands.
  5. Political / election cycle (weight 15%) \u2014 historically ASUU avoids strikes in the 90 days before a major federal election but ramps up immediately after.
  6. FX / inflation real-wage erosion (weight 15%) \u2014 when CBN-published inflation > 25% for 3 consecutive months, real-wage anger amplifies every other signal.

All inputs sourced from public ASUU communiqu\u00e9s, FG press releases, CBN bulletins, and reputable Nigerian press. Each weekly update is timestamped and archived; we publish revisions to the methodology openly.
