EduPlanner

Role-based task planner for Nigerian educators — stay organised with pre-loaded daily, weekly, and termly tasks tailored to your school role.

What It Does

EduPlanner is a free, open-source task management web app built specifically for educators in Nigerian schools. It provides 6 role-based modules — each pre-loaded with the daily, weekly, and termly tasks that role actually handles. Teachers, administrators, HODs, ICT coordinators, librarians, and school counsellors can track progress, add custom tasks, and export their task reports as PDF or Excel files.

For Teachers

Track lesson planning, attendance, grade submissions, parent meetings, and professional development — all pre-loaded and ready to check off.

For Administrators

Manage staff coordination, facility checks, compliance reporting, budget oversight, and communication with parents and stakeholders.

For All School Roles

HODs, ICT Coordinators, Librarians, and Counsellors each get their own module with tasks specific to their responsibilities in a Nigerian school context.

Features

Everything You Need to Stay Organised

Built with the realities of Nigerian schools in mind — no internet required after first load, no accounts to create, no fees to pay.

6 Role Modules

Teacher, Administrator, Head of Department, ICT Coordinator, Librarian, and School Counsellor — each with pre-populated tasks.

Task Management

Check off tasks, add custom ones, delete tasks, clear completed items, and track progress with a visual progress bar.

PDF & Excel Export

Generate branded task reports as PDF (via print view) or download formatted Excel spreadsheets with role, date, and task status.

Works Offline

Progressive Web App with service worker — install on your phone or desktop and use it without an internet connection.

Dark Mode

Auto-detects your system theme preference with a manual toggle. Easy on the eyes during late-night planning sessions.

Free & Open Source

MIT Licensed. No accounts, no subscriptions, no data collection. Self-host it, fork it, or contribute to the codebase on GitHub.
